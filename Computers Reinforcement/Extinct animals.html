Animal Extinction

"The love for all animals is the most noble attribute of a human" - Charles Darwin, Geologist



Animals have been on Earth from about 15 billion years. They have evolved from being paleontologic and simple creatures to vast, complex species. However, due to extreme temparature changes, meteor strikes and other catalystic events, some of these species have gone extinct

Extinction of a particular animal or plant species occurs when there are no more individuals of that species alive anywhere in the world - the species has died out. This is a natural part of evolution. But sometimes extinctions happen at a much faster rate than usual.

For example, we all have heard about the extinction of dinosaurs. These were a dominant predator species, but they went extinct because of meteor strikes and other temperature changes. But, for example, the Dodo went extinct because of habitat loss, over harvesting and loss of suitable food.

However these aren't the only reasons of extinction. There are many reasons for extinction and many of them are caused by humans. Habitat conversion climate change, and over hunting. Almost 178 species of animals have gone extinct because of humans. For examples:-

  • Stellar Sea Cow
  • Labrador Duck
  • Tasmanian Wolf
  • Cisco Fish
  • Golden Toad
  • Thylacine

Endngered species are the animals who are in the danger of getting extinct. Examples of endangered species are Bengal Tiger, Blackbuck, Panda And Blue Whale. multiple reasons are behind the endangerment of these species, but the main reason behind this is over extinction and poaching. If not protected by strong laws and Sanctuaries, these animals may soon go extinct
